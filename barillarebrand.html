Barilla Rebrand Project

Project Overview - March - May, 2024 / Brand Redesign / Brand - Barilla / Current Demographic: 25-35 / Redesign demographic: Gen z (16-24)

Software used - Adobe illustrator, Adobe photoshop, Adobe firefly

Challenge - To redesign a classic and age old brand “barilla”, to look like a new brand marketed for a younger, newer generation

Solution - Hold onto colours to keep the recognition and play around with the typeface and merchandising

This is a brand redesign project I completed during my freshman year for the "Product, Promotion, and Packaging" class. The goal was to give an evergreen brand like Barilla a fresh look from a Gen Z perspective. The original brand colors were retained to ensure recognizability on the shelf, while the updated font, logo, and packaging were designed to appeal to college students aged 18-25.

For the logo redesign, I decided to keep the original colors after careful consideration. Initially, I chose existing typefaces that resonate with Gen Z, but eventually, I sketched my own design, scanned it, and digitized it in Illustrator. I also introduced a mascot for the brand, resulting in whimsical packaging with different characters corresponding to various pasta shapes, inspired by Barilla’s “Intensely Italian” campaign. Additionally, I added fun elements to the side panels of the packaging to make it stand out further on the shelf.

For the merchandise design, I maintained a similar style but aimed for a more generic, useable look. I achieved this with a retro poster featuring a homely bowl of pasta. For social media campaigns, I researched Gen Z interactions and found that they engage with filters and challenges. This inspired me to create a "Guess the Pasta" filter, launching the #BarillaChallenge.
