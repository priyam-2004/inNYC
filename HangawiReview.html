Hangawi: A review of one of my favoutites

Tucked away in the heart of Manhattan's Koreatown, Hangawi offers an extraordinary dining experience that transports you far from the hustle and bustle of New York City. This serene, vegetarian Korean temple cuisine restaurant is not just about food; it's about creating a mindful, meditative experience that engages all the senses. From the moment you step inside, you’re invited to leave your shoes at the door, heightening the sense of calm and intimacy. The interior is designed to evoke a traditional Korean setting, with earthy tones, wooden accents, and soft lighting creating an ambiance of tranquility. The low tables and floor seating further encourage a connected dining experience, making you feel grounded and present. The menu at Hangawi is entirely vegetarian, with many vegan and gluten-free options. Yet, the creativity and depth of flavors here can convert even the most dedicated carnivores. Every dish feels like it has been carefully crafted, honoring the ingredients and the heritage of Korean temple food. I started with the Emperor’s Rolls, filled with tofu, shiitake mushrooms, and vegetables, which were light yet packed with rich umami flavors. For the main course, the Organic Zen Bibimbap was a standout. Served in a sizzling stone pot, it featured a vibrant array of fresh vegetables and crispy rice, enhanced by a perfectly balanced gochujang sauce. The addition of organic tofu gave it a protein boost without overwhelming the delicate flavors of the dish. A must-try is the Stone Bowl Tofu Kimchi Stew, a comforting, spicy dish that feels both hearty and healthy, the tang of the kimchi perfectly cutting through the richness of the stew. Paired with their house-made tea, it felt like a nourishing, warming meal ideal for any season. The desserts are equally noteworthy. The Black Sesame Tofu Pudding was surprisingly decadent, with a silky texture and just the right hint of sweetness, while the Sweet Red Bean Pancakes were crisp on the outside and soft on the inside, offering a lovely end to the meal. Beyond the food, Hangawi is an oasis of calm. The attentive yet unobtrusive service adds to the feeling that this is a place of peaceful escape. It's a place to slow down, savor each bite, and enjoy the company you're with, making it a perfect spot for a quiet date night or a relaxing dinner with friends. While Hangawi isn’t the cheapest dining option in Koreatown, it’s worth every penny for the high quality of ingredients, presentation, and ambiance. For anyone looking to experience a different side of Korean cuisine or simply seeking a moment of Zen in the city, Hangawi offers a perfect retreat.
